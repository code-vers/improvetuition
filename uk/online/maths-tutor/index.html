06 / Questions

Online Maths tuition FAQs.

Is the assessment really free?

Yes. The Maths assessment is free, with no payment and no obligation. You only decide about paid tuition afterwards, once you have a clear picture of where your child is losing marks.

What if I do not know what my child is struggling with?

That is exactly what the assessment is for. You do not need to diagnose the problem. You only need to know your child is stuck — we find the specific gap and explain it clearly.

How does online tuition work?

Lessons are live online using a shared screen and digital whiteboard. The assessment and tuition direction are teacher-led and guided by Mr G Dabhad, QTS, and lessons are delivered by our DBS-checked Maths tutors. You receive a link before each session.

What ages and stages do you cover?

We teach Maths from Year 2 through Year 13 — primary (KS1–KS2), secondary (KS3), GCSE (Years 10–11), and A-Level (Years 12–13). SATs preparation at Year 2 and Year 6 is included.

Is online Maths tuition suitable for a child who lacks confidence?

Yes. Many pupils come to us having lost confidence. Because our online Maths tutoring assesses first and teaches from the right starting point, lessons feel achievable rather than overwhelming, which rebuilds confidence over time.

How much does online Maths tuition cost?

Year 2 to GCSE: £14.50 per hour in a small group (4–5 pupils), £22.50 in a trio (3 pupils), or £39.50 one-to-one. A-Level (Sixth Form): £18.50 per hour in a group of 4–5, or £45 one-to-one. We recommend the right format after the assessment. The assessment itself is always free.

Who leads the assessment and tuition plan?

The assessment and tuition plan are teacher-led and guided by Mr G Dabhad, QTS, founder of Improve Tuition since 2007. Lessons are delivered by DBS-checked tutors working to that plan.

Which exam boards do you cover for GCSE and A-Level Maths?

We cover the main UK exam boards, including AQA, Edexcel and OCR. Tuition is matched to your child's specific board and tier, so practice questions and exam technique reflect the papers they will actually sit.

Does online Maths tuition actually work?

Yes. Lessons are live and interactive, using a shared screen and digital whiteboard so your child works through problems with the tutor in real time, just as they would in person. Because we assess first and teach from the right starting point, online lessons stay focused on the specific gaps holding your child back.

Do you offer Maths tuition near me in Batley?

Yes. As well as teaching online across the UK, we offer in-person Maths tuition at our Batley centre on George Street, serving families across Dewsbury, Birstall, Heckmondwike and the wider Kirklees area. The free assessment is the same whether you choose online or in-person lessons.
