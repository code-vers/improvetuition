02 / What May Be Happening

Same low mark. Different cause.

Two children can get the same Chemistry score for completely different reasons.

  • One may understand the chemistry but be blocked by the maths behind moles and calculations — rearranging, units, ratios.
  • Another may have a shaky grasp of bonding and structure, so later topics never quite hold.
  • A third may be lost in organic mechanisms and pathways — unable to see how one reaction leads to the next.

More revision guides won’t fix a problem we haven’t named yet.

07 / Questions

The questions parents ask most.

Does online Chemistry tuition actually work?

Yes. Lessons are live and one-to-one, not recorded videos. Tutor and pupil share a screen and a digital whiteboard, so equations, mole calculations, structures and organic mechanisms are worked through together in real time, exactly as they would be on paper. The tutor sees your child's working as it happens and corrects it on the spot.

What does my child need to join an online lesson?

A laptop, tablet or computer with a webcam and a stable internet connection is enough to start. A second device or a graphics tablet can help with writing out workings, but is not required. We send a simple link to join — there is nothing complicated to install, and we help you set up before the first lesson.

Can the tutor really see my child's working in Chemistry?

Yes, and this is the part parents worry about most. Through the shared whiteboard your child writes out calculations and mechanisms live, and the tutor watches the working unfold rather than just seeing a final answer. That is how we catch the exact step where a mole calculation or a mechanism goes wrong.

Is the free assessment online too?

Yes. The assessment is a short, guided online Chemistry check over a shared screen. There is no payment to book it and no obligation to take tuition afterwards. We use it to find your child's gaps and explain the clearest next step before any paid lessons begin.

Do you cover Chemistry within Combined Science as well as Triple?

Yes. We support Chemistry as part of GCSE Combined Science (AQA Trilogy and equivalent double awards) and as a separate GCSE in Triple Science. Online tuition is matched to your child's specification and tier.

My child struggles with the maths in Chemistry. Can you help online?

Yes, and it is one of the most common reasons pupils lose Chemistry marks. Moles, concentrations, rearranging equations, percentage yield and titration calculations all rely on secure maths. The online assessment identifies whether the barrier is chemical understanding or the underlying maths, and lessons target whichever is holding your child back.

How much does online Chemistry tuition cost?

Online GCSE Chemistry tuition starts from £14.50 per hour in a group of 4–5, £22.50 in a small group of 2–3, or £39.50 one-to-one. Online A Level Chemistry is £18.50 per hour in a group of 4–5, or one-to-one from £45 per hour — exact rate confirmed after the free assessment.
