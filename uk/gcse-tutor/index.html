When hard work is not turning into marks

Many parents come to Improve Tuition when Maths starts to feel serious — often in Year 10 or 11, when mocks are lower than expected and time feels short.

The problem is rarely effort. The marks drift because the real cause has not been found.

Built for pupils who are trying, but still losing marks

This page exists for one kind of pupil — the one who is working hard at Maths but still losing marks. Our job is to find why, explain it clearly, and rebuild the marks back up before the exam.

Often it's one specific gap.

The patterns we see most often when marks are uneven across the paper.

  • 01 Shaky foundations — times tables, fractions, decimals, percentages
  • 02 Algebra — equations, brackets, simultaneous equations
  • 03 Multi-step questions where they do not know how to begin
  • 04 Foundation or Higher tier — and parents wanting a clearer view

Why the Maths gap should be found before tuition starts.

More teaching is not always the same as better teaching.

With assessment first

04 / How tuition follows

After the assessment, tuition follows the plan.

The assessment is not the end point. It gives the order for what should be fixed first.

Step one

Secure the foundations.

If number fluency, fractions or basic algebra are weak, we deal with those first so later exam questions become easier to handle.

Step two

Build exam marks.

Once the basics are stronger, we work on exam-question reading, method marks, multi-step questions and confidence under pressure.
