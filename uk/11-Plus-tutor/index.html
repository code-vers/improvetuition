Every board has its own character.

The 11 Plus is not a single exam. It is a family of assessments — each with its own format, style and rhythm. Preparation that ignores the difference does children a disservice. Here is what we cover.

What we cover

Four strands. Every strand built up carefully.

Every 11 Plus paper draws on a combination of these four. We work each one as its own discipline.

Strand 01

English

Comprehension is the foundation — reading closely, inferring meaning, picking up tone, and working with unfamiliar vocabulary under timed conditions. Spelling, punctuation, grammar and creative or persuasive writing follow where the assessment requires it.

Topics: comprehension, vocabulary, SPaG, creative writing, persuasive writing, tone & inference

Strand 02

Maths

11 Plus Maths goes beyond standard Key Stage 2 content. Multi-step problems, mental arithmetic under timing, fractions, ratios and percentages, and applied reasoning all appear. We build fluency first, then layer in exam strategy.

Topics: number, fractions & decimals, percentages, ratio, algebra, geometry, data, multi-step problem solving

Strand 03

Verbal Reasoning

Verbal Reasoning is its own discipline. Children meet 21 classic GL question types — coded words, letter sequences, analogies, antonyms, synonyms, hidden words and more. Familiarity with each type, alongside strong vocabulary, is what moves scores.

Topics: cloze, coded words, letter sequences, analogies, antonyms & synonyms, word categories, logic puzzles

Strand 04

Non-Verbal Reasoning

Non-Verbal Reasoning works with shapes, patterns and spatial relationships rather than words. The question types are systematic — rotations, reflections, odd-one-out, series, matrices, nets and 3D shapes. Practice on each type builds visual fluency.

Topics: odd-one-out, series, matrices, rotations, reflections, analogies, nets, 3D shape, codes

06 / Questions

The questions parents ask most.

What is the 11 Plus and which assessment boards exist?

The 11 Plus is the entrance exam used by grammar schools and many independent schools for Year 7 entry. The two main boards are CEM (Centre for Evaluation and Monitoring) and GL Assessment. Independent schools often use the ISEB Common Pre-Test, and some set their own bespoke papers. Papers test English, Maths, Verbal Reasoning and Non-Verbal Reasoning in varying combinations.

What is the difference between CEM and GL Assessment?

CEM papers tend to mix subjects within a single test and emphasise vocabulary and speed under pressure. GL Assessment uses separate papers for each subject with a more predictable question style. Preparation strategies differ — we tailor lessons to the format used by your child’s target school.

What is the ISEB Common Pre-Test?

The ISEB Common Pre-Test is an online, adaptive assessment used by many leading independent schools as part of Year 7 (and sometimes Year 9) entry. It covers English, Maths, Verbal Reasoning and Non-Verbal Reasoning. Because it is adaptive, question difficulty adjusts to the child’s responses, which makes pacing and accuracy under timed conditions essential.

When should 11 Plus preparation begin?

Most families begin in Year 4, giving sixteen to eighteen months of paced preparation before assessments in the autumn of Year 6. Where families come to us later, we build a tailored intensive plan focused on the specific board and target school.

Do you prepare children for independent school entrance exams?

Yes. We support preparation for the ISEB Common Pre-Test and for the bespoke entrance papers used by individual independent schools — including English comprehension, creative writing, Maths and reasoning sections. We discuss your target school during the free assessment and tailor lessons to its specific format.

Is the 11 Plus assessment really free?

Yes. There is no payment to book the assessment and no obligation to take tuition afterwards. We use it to find your child’s gaps, confirm the right preparation route, and explain the clearest next step before any paid lessons begin.
