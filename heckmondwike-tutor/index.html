What we prepare

Four areas. Every area built up carefully.

The Heckmondwike Grammar FSCE tests four things together. We work each one as its own discipline.

Strand 01

English

Reading comprehension under timed conditions, inferring meaning from unfamiliar passages, spelling, punctuation and grammar. The English paper looks for children who can read closely and answer carefully — not children who rush.

Areas: comprehension · vocabulary · SPaG · tone & inference · close reading

Strand 02

Maths

Based on Key Stage 2 knowledge, but tested through more demanding reasoning, timing and problem-solving. Multi-step questions, mental arithmetic under pressure, fractions, ratios, percentages and applied reasoning all matter. We build fluency first, then layer in exam strategy.

Areas: number · fractions & decimals · percentages · ratio · algebra · geometry · data · multi-step problem solving

Strand 03

Creative Writing

Creative writing tests imagination, original thinking and critical problem-solving. It asks children to plan and produce a structured piece under timed conditions — narrative, descriptive or persuasive — demonstrating the range of writing that secondary school will demand.

Areas: narrative writing · descriptive writing · persuasive writing · planning under time · openings & endings · vocabulary range

Strand 04

Exam Technique

The discipline of the paper itself. Time management, knowing when to move on, checking work carefully, and handling the question that doesn’t look familiar. Often the difference between a near-miss and a place.

Areas: time management · question prioritisation · checking technique · handling unfamiliar questions · managing pressure

03 / Questions

The questions parents ask most.

What does the Heckmondwike Grammar entrance exam involve?

The Year 7 entrance assessment (FSCE) covers English, Maths and Creative Writing, with exam technique woven through. It is set by the school and used to identify children who are ready for the academic demands of Heckmondwike Grammar. Specific format and dates change year to year — always check the school’s official admissions page for the latest information.

What is the FSCE?

The FSCE is the Year 7 entrance assessment set by Heckmondwike Grammar School. Our preparation is tailored to the paper your child will sit — building underlying skills first, then layering in exam technique.

When should preparation begin?

Most families begin in late Year 4 or early Year 5, giving twelve to sixteen months of focused, paced preparation before the assessment in Year 6. Where time is shorter, we build a tailored intensive plan around your child.

Where are lessons held?

Lessons take place at our Batley centre, with online lessons available for families who prefer remote support. Both formats follow the same structured plan and the same teacher-led approach.

How will I know if my child is ready?

The free assessment is designed to answer exactly this. We look at where your child is across English, Maths and Creative Writing, identify the specific gaps that would cost marks, and explain honestly whether tuition would help — and what the plan would look like.

Is the assessment really free?

Yes. No payment to book, no obligation to continue. We find what’s costing marks, explain the next step — that’s it.

How much does Heckmondwike Grammar tuition cost?

From £14.50/hr in a group of 4–5 · £22.50/hr small group of 2–3 · £39.50/hr one-to-one. Exact rate confirmed after the free assessment.
